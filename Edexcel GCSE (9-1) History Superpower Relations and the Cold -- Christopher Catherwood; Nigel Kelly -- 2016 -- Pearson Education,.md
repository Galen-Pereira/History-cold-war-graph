# Edexcel GCSE (9-1) History Superpower Relations and the Cold War — Event List

This file lists only dated Cold War events with concise detail.

## Events

### Mar 5, 1946 — Iron Curtain Speech
- **Summary:** Winston Churchill warns that an 'Iron Curtain' has descended across Europe. The speech frames Soviet expansion as a long-term strategic threat and hardens Western attitudes.
- **Tension:** 5.0/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Mar 12, 1947 — Truman Doctrine
- **Summary:** Truman declares US will contain Soviet expansion. Greece & Turkey receive $400M. Cold War containment framework formally established before Congress.
- **Tension:** 5.0/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** US

### Mar 12, 1947 — Truman Doctrine (Aid Approved)
- **Summary:** Congress approves major aid for Greece and Turkey under Truman's containment policy. Washington commits to sustained anti-communist intervention outside the Western Hemisphere.
- **Tension:** 5.2/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jun 5, 1947 — Marshall Plan Announced
- **Summary:** Marshall proposes massive economic aid to rebuild Europe. USSR refuses and pressures Eastern Bloc to reject it. The economic iron curtain deepens.
- **Tension:** 4.5/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** US

### Sep 18, 1947 — CIA Created / Cominform Founded
- **Summary:** USSR founds Cominform to coordinate communist parties. US creates the CIA under the National Security Act. Both superpowers build covert intelligence infrastructure.
- **Tension:** 5.2/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Feb 25, 1948 — Communist Coup — Czechoslovakia
- **Summary:** Soviet-backed communists seize power in Prague. Democratic government ousted. Shocks Western Europe and accelerates NATO negotiations.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Feb 25, 1948 — Czechoslovak Non-Communist Ministers Resign
- **Summary:** A constitutional crisis in Prague allows communists, backed by Soviet pressure, to consolidate control. The event shocks Western governments and public opinion.
- **Tension:** 6.2/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Mar 17, 1948 — Brussels Treaty Signed
- **Summary:** Britain, France, Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg form a collective defense pact. It becomes a direct precursor to NATO's military architecture.
- **Tension:** 5.8/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jun 20, 1948 — Deutsche Mark Introduced in West Zones
- **Summary:** Western occupation authorities launch currency reform in western Germany. Moscow views it as unilateral state-building and escalates pressure in Berlin.
- **Tension:** 6.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jun 24, 1948 — Berlin Blockade
- **Summary:** Stalin blockades West Berlin — 2 million civilians cut off. A massive US airlift lasting 323 days delivers 2.3M tons of supplies. First major Cold War confrontation.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Apr 4, 1949 — NATO Founded
- **Summary:** North Atlantic Treaty signed. 12 nations form a collective defense pact. Stalin views it as direct military encirclement of the USSR.
- **Tension:** 7.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### May 12, 1949 — Berlin Blockade Lifted
- **Summary:** Moscow ends the land blockade after the sustained Western airlift. The confrontation confirms that coercion over Berlin can trigger major allied counter-mobilization.
- **Tension:** 6.8/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### May 23, 1949 — Federal Republic of Germany Formed
- **Summary:** West Germany is established from the western occupation zones. The institutional division of Germany becomes entrenched in Cold War geopolitics.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Aug 29, 1949 — USSR Detonates First A-Bomb
- **Summary:** Joe-1 detonated — USSR breaks the US nuclear monopoly 4 years ahead of Western estimates. Truman orders H-bomb development. The arms race begins.
- **Tension:** 8.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Oct 1, 1949 — China Falls to Communism
- **Summary:** Mao's PLA wins civil war. Nationalist government flees to Taiwan. The communist bloc now encompasses 1/4 of humanity.
- **Tension:** 8.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Oct 7, 1949 — German Democratic Republic Formed
- **Summary:** East Germany is proclaimed in the Soviet zone as a response to West German statehood. Rival German regimes formalize the front line of Europe.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Jan 14, 1950 — Sino-Soviet Treaty of Friendship
- **Summary:** Mao and Stalin sign a treaty of alliance and assistance, tightening the communist bloc at the start of the Korean War era.
- **Tension:** 7.2/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jan 30, 1950 — NSC-68 — Nuclear Buildup Directive
- **Summary:** Top-secret NSC-68 calls for quadrupling US defense spending. Becomes the blueprint for US Cold War strategy for decades.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jun 25, 1950 — Korean War Begins
- **Summary:** North Korea invades South Korea with Soviet-supplied tanks. US leads UN forces. MacArthur pushes to the Yalu River. China sends 300,000 troops. Brutal stalemate.
- **Tension:** 8.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Apr 11, 1951 — Truman Dismisses General MacArthur
- **Summary:** Truman removes MacArthur for publicly challenging civilian strategy in Korea. The decision confirms limited-war doctrine and civilian control in a nuclear age.
- **Tension:** 6.6/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jun 23, 1951 — Korean Armistice Talks Open
- **Summary:** Negotiations begin at Kaesong after a year of heavy fighting. The talks drag on while combat continues, revealing limits of military victory.
- **Tension:** 6.7/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Nov 1, 1952 — US Tests Hydrogen Bomb
- **Summary:** Ivy Mike — first thermonuclear device, 10.4 megatons, 700× Hiroshima. Escalates destructive capacity to civilization-ending scale.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Mar 5, 1953 — Stalin Dies
- **Summary:** Stalin dies — power struggle erupts in the Kremlin. Beria arrested and shot. Khrushchev emerges. CIA considers exploiting the transition.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Jul 27, 1953 — Korean Armistice Signed
- **Summary:** Korean War ends exactly where it started, at the 38th Parallel. 36,500 Americans, 900,000 Chinese, millions of Koreans dead. A frozen conflict persisting today.
- **Tension:** 6.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Aug 12, 1953 — USSR Tests H-Bomb
- **Summary:** Joe-4 detonated — just 9 months after the US. Khrushchev now inherits an arsenal capable of reaching any American city.
- **Tension:** 7.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Jun 9, 1954 — Army-McCarthy Hearings
- **Summary:** 'Have you no sense of decency?' McCarthy censured by Senate in December. Domestic anti-communist hysteria begins to recede.
- **Tension:** 5.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Aug 19, 1954 — CIA Coups — Iran & Guatemala
- **Summary:** CIA overthrows Mosaddegh in Iran (Op. Ajax) and Árbenz in Guatemala (PBSUCCESS). Covert Cold War expands into the developing world.
- **Tension:** 5.8/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### May 9, 1955 — West Germany Joins NATO
- **Summary:** The Federal Republic of Germany enters NATO, deepening Soviet fears of rearmed West German alignment with the US-led bloc.
- **Tension:** 6.9/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### May 14, 1955 — Austria State Treaty
- **Summary:** Occupation forces withdraw and Austria adopts permanent neutrality. It is a rare negotiated settlement between the blocs during high confrontation.
- **Tension:** 4.8/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### May 14, 1955 — Warsaw Pact Founded
- **Summary:** USSR and 7 Eastern Bloc states sign the Warsaw Pact — direct military counter to NATO. Europe formally divided into two armed blocs.
- **Tension:** 5.0/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Jul 18, 1955 — Geneva Summit — Spirit of Geneva
- **Summary:** First superpower summit since Potsdam. Eisenhower proposes 'Open Skies' aerial inspection — USSR refuses. Brief optimism. Austria becomes neutral.
- **Tension:** 4.5/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Feb 25, 1956 — Khrushchev's Secret Speech
- **Summary:** Khrushchev denounces Stalin's crimes to the Communist Party congress. Shock waves through the Eastern Bloc. Signals possible liberalization.
- **Tension:** 5.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Oct 23, 1956 — Hungarian Uprising Begins
- **Summary:** Mass protests in Budapest demand reform and Soviet troop withdrawal. The revolt briefly threatens Soviet control before military intervention restores dominance.
- **Tension:** 7.2/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Oct 29, 1956 — Suez Crisis
- **Summary:** UK, France & Israel invade Egypt. Both US and USSR demand withdrawal. Britain humiliated — the end of European imperial power.
- **Tension:** 7.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Oct 30, 1956 — Imre Nagy Announces Hungary Leaving Warsaw Pact
- **Summary:** Nagy declares neutrality and withdrawal from the Warsaw Pact during the Hungarian uprising. Moscow interprets this as a direct challenge to bloc control.
- **Tension:** 8.3/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Nov 4, 1956 — Hungarian Revolution Crushed
- **Summary:** Khrushchev sends 1,000+ tanks into Budapest. 2,500 Hungarians killed. UN condemns the USSR. The West watches helplessly.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Nov 4, 1956 — Soviet Troops Re-enter Hungary
- **Summary:** Large Soviet armored formations enter Budapest to depose Nagy's government. The operation reimposes communist rule and crushes resistance.
- **Tension:** 8.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Oct 4, 1957 — Sputnik — Space Race Begins
- **Summary:** USSR launches Sputnik — the first satellite. The bleeping signal panics America. Proves USSR has ICBM capability to hit anywhere on Earth.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Nov 3, 1957 — Sputnik 2 / Laika
- **Summary:** Dog Laika orbits Earth. US scrambles to respond. Eisenhower creates NASA and DARPA. The Vanguard rocket explodes live on American television.
- **Tension:** 6.8/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Nov 10, 1958 — Khrushchev Berlin Deadline Speech
- **Summary:** Soviet leadership reiterates demands for a separate peace treaty and Western withdrawal timetable from Berlin, intensifying the city crisis.
- **Tension:** 6.6/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Nov 27, 1958 — Berlin Ultimatum
- **Summary:** Khrushchev demands Western powers vacate Berlin within 6 months. US refuses. Berlin becomes Cold War's most dangerous flashpoint.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Jan 1, 1959 — Cuban Revolution — Castro Takes Power
- **Summary:** Castro ousts Batista. US ambivalence shifts to hostility as Castro nationalizes American assets. USSR begins supplying arms to Cuba — 90 miles from Florida.
- **Tension:** 5.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jul 24, 1959 — Camp David Talks (Eisenhower–Khrushchev Process)
- **Summary:** Following Khrushchev's US visit, leaders agree to continue dialogue and postpone the Berlin deadline. The temporary thaw raises hopes but remains fragile.
- **Tension:** 4.6/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jul 24, 1959 — Kitchen Debate
- **Summary:** Nixon and Khrushchev debate capitalism vs. communism in a model American kitchen in Moscow. A televised ideological clash for the ages.
- **Tension:** 5.0/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### May 1, 1960 — U-2 Spy Plane Shot Down
- **Summary:** Gary Powers shot down over Sverdlovsk. Eisenhower caught lying. Paris Summit collapses. The Cold War thaw freezes solid.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Nov 8, 1960 — Kennedy Elected
- **Summary:** JFK wins on closing the 'missile gap' (later found to heavily favor the US). Khrushchev initially believes he can exploit Kennedy's inexperience.
- **Tension:** 7.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Apr 17, 1961 — Bay of Pigs — Catastrophic Failure
- **Summary:** CIA-trained Cuban exiles invade — Kennedy refuses air cover. 1,200 captured. Kennedy is humiliated. Khrushchev resolves to place missiles in Cuba.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### May 5, 1961 — Vienna Summit Preparatory NATO Consultations
- **Summary:** US and allies coordinate positions ahead of Kennedy's meeting with Khrushchev, anticipating renewed pressure over Berlin.
- **Tension:** 6.4/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jun 3, 1961 — Kennedy Announces Expanded Conventional Build-Up
- **Summary:** After Vienna tensions, Kennedy requests major increases in US conventional and civil defense capabilities. Washington prepares for prolonged Berlin and global confrontation.
- **Tension:** 7.1/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jun 4, 1961 — Vienna Summit — Khrushchev Bullies JFK
- **Summary:** Khrushchev intimidates the 44-year-old Kennedy, renews the Berlin ultimatum. JFK reportedly shaken: 'It's going to be a cold winter.'
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Aug 13, 1961 — Berlin Wall Constructed
- **Summary:** East Germany erects the Wall overnight. Barbed wire first, then concrete. Families divided. Kennedy calls it 'a wall of shame' but accepts it over war.
- **Tension:** 8.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Aug 15, 1961 — Border Closures Harden Around Berlin
- **Summary:** East German authorities convert initial wire barriers into fortified controls and armed checkpoints, making division effectively permanent.
- **Tension:** 8.1/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Oct 27, 1961 — Checkpoint Charlie Tank Standoff
- **Summary:** US and Soviet tanks face each other at close range in Berlin. Diplomatic backchannels avert live fire after hours of confrontation.
- **Tension:** 8.7/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Oct 30, 1961 — Tsar Bomba — 50 Megatons
- **Summary:** The most powerful explosion in human history. Shockwave circles Earth three times. Windows shattered 900km away. Pure nuclear intimidation.
- **Tension:** 8.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Mar 1, 1962 — US Nuclear Superiority Revealed
- **Summary:** US official reveals a massive lead in warheads — destroying Khrushchev's claims of parity. Humiliated, he secretly resolves to place missiles in Cuba.
- **Tension:** 8.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Oct 16, 1962 — Cuban Missile Crisis — DEFCON 2
- **Summary:** October 16–28: U-2 photos reveal Soviet missiles in Cuba. DEFCON 2. 13 days of secret negotiations. Soviet ships turn back. Missiles withdrawn. The world's closest approach to nuclear annihilation.
- **Tension:** 10.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Oct 24, 1962 — US Naval Quarantine Line Enforced
- **Summary:** US forces begin strict interception around Cuba under quarantine rules. Multiple Soviet vessels alter course as crisis diplomacy intensifies.
- **Tension:** 9.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Oct 27, 1962 — Black Saturday — Closest Point of Cuban Missile Crisis
- **Summary:** A U-2 is shot down over Cuba and another strays into Soviet airspace. Field commanders and political leaders face minutes-to-midnight nuclear escalation risks.
- **Tension:** 10.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jun 20, 1963 — Moscow Hotline Established
- **Summary:** Direct teletype link between the White House and Kremlin installed after the missile crisis. First use during the 1967 Six-Day War.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jun 30, 1963 — Hotline Agreement Signed
- **Summary:** Washington and Moscow sign the agreement creating direct crisis communication links. The mechanism aims to reduce accidental war from miscalculation.
- **Tension:** 6.2/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Aug 5, 1963 — Partial Nuclear Test Ban Treaty
- **Summary:** US, USSR & UK ban atmospheric, underwater and space nuclear tests. First arms control agreement of the Cold War.
- **Tension:** 6.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Nov 22, 1963 — Kennedy Assassinated
- **Summary:** JFK shot in Dallas. Soviets briefly feared it was a prelude to a US attack and went on alert. A Cold War leadership vacuum at a critical moment.
- **Tension:** 7.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Aug 7, 1964 — Gulf of Tonkin — Vietnam Escalates
- **Summary:** Alleged North Vietnamese attacks on US destroyers. Congress issues the Gulf of Tonkin Resolution — a blank check for war. Later found manipulated.
- **Tension:** 6.8/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Oct 14, 1964 — Khrushchev Ousted
- **Summary:** Khrushchev removed by the Politburo — humiliated over Cuba, agricultural failures, erratic behavior. Brezhnev takes power. A harder line returns.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Oct 16, 1964 — China Detonates Atomic Bomb
- **Summary:** PRC tests its first nuclear device. Sino-Soviet split now encompasses nuclear dimension. A three-way Cold War dynamic emerges.
- **Tension:** 7.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Mar 8, 1965 — Vietnam War Escalation
- **Summary:** Operation Rolling Thunder. Marines land at Da Nang. 184,000 US troops by year's end; 536,000 by 1968. USSR and China supply North Vietnam.
- **Tension:** 6.8/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jun 5, 1967 — Six-Day War
- **Summary:** Israel defeats Egypt, Syria, Jordan in 6 days. US backs Israel; USSR backs the Arabs. The Moscow-Washington hotline is used for the first time.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jun 10, 1967 — Glassboro Summit (Johnson–Kosygin)
- **Summary:** US and Soviet leaders hold emergency talks during the Arab-Israeli war. Though no formal deal emerges, dialogue helps cap direct superpower risk.
- **Tension:** 6.1/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jan 30, 1968 — Tet Offensive — Vietnam Turning Point
- **Summary:** North Vietnam launches massive coordinated attacks. US Embassy in Saigon breached. A military setback absorbed, but American public support shatters.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Mar 31, 1968 — Johnson Limits Vietnam Bombing
- **Summary:** LBJ halts most bombing of North Vietnam and opens a path to negotiations. The move reflects strategic strain and domestic pressure on US policy.
- **Tension:** 6.1/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jul 1, 1968 — NPT Opened for Signature
- **Summary:** The Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty is opened in Washington, London, and Moscow to limit spread of nuclear weapons beyond existing powers.
- **Tension:** 5.7/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Aug 20, 1968 — Prague Spring Crushed
- **Summary:** Dubček's reforms crushed by 500,000 Warsaw Pact troops. The Brezhnev Doctrine: USSR can intervene anywhere socialism is threatened.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Jan 20, 1969 — Nixon Inauguration and Vietnamization Signal
- **Summary:** The new US administration signals a shift toward reducing direct ground burden while maintaining anti-communist containment commitments.
- **Tension:** 5.9/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Mar 2, 1969 — Sino-Soviet Border Clashes
- **Summary:** Nuclear powers exchanging fire along the Ussuri River. Soviets discuss a pre-emptive strike on China. Nixon sees a strategic opportunity.
- **Tension:** 5.8/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Jul 20, 1969 — Moon Landing — Apollo 11
- **Summary:** Neil Armstrong walks on the Moon. Decisive US victory in the Space Race. The Soviet lunar program is secretly abandoned.
- **Tension:** 5.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### May 4, 1970 — Kent State / Cambodia Invasion
- **Summary:** US expands the war into Cambodia. National Guard kills 4 students at Kent State. 450 colleges shut down. US Cold War credibility erodes.
- **Tension:** 6.2/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Jul 9, 1971 — Ping-Pong Diplomacy / China Opening
- **Summary:** Kissinger secretly visits Beijing. Nixon prepares his historic opening. Triangular diplomacy — playing China against the USSR — begins.
- **Tension:** 5.0/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** US

### Feb 21, 1972 — Nixon Visits China
- **Summary:** Nixon lands in Beijing — the week that changed the world. Meets Mao. Fundamentally realigns global Cold War balance. USSR is deeply alarmed.
- **Tension:** 4.8/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** US

### May 26, 1972 — Basic Principles Agreement (US–USSR)
- **Summary:** Alongside SALT I, both powers sign principles for coexistence and crisis restraint. It codifies détente norms even as rivalry continues in the periphery.
- **Tension:** 4.4/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### May 26, 1972 — SALT I / ABM Treaty — Détente Peak
- **Summary:** Nixon visits Moscow. SALT I caps nuclear delivery vehicles. ABM Treaty limits missile defense. Détente at its zenith.
- **Tension:** 4.5/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Sep 11, 1973 — Chile Coup — CIA Backs Pinochet
- **Summary:** CIA spends $8M destabilizing Chile. Allende killed. Pinochet's brutal dictatorship installed. The Latin American left radicalizes.
- **Tension:** 5.2/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Oct 6, 1973 — Yom Kippur War / DEFCON 3
- **Summary:** Egypt and Syria attack Israel. When the USSR threatens direct intervention, the US raises alert to DEFCON 3 — the closest nuclear standoff since Cuba.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Aug 9, 1974 — Nixon Resigns — Watergate
- **Summary:** Nixon resigns — crippled US foreign policy. Kissinger maintains Détente but loses political backing. CIA operations curtailed by Congress.
- **Tension:** 5.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Apr 30, 1975 — Saigon Falls
- **Summary:** North Vietnamese tanks roll into Saigon. Chaotic US evacuation from the embassy roof. 58,000 Americans died. Communism appears to be advancing globally.
- **Tension:** 5.0/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### May 1, 1975 — Mayaguez Incident
- **Summary:** US forces launch an operation after Cambodian seizure of the SS Mayaguez. The clash highlights post-Vietnam volatility in US Cold War posture.
- **Tension:** 6.2/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** US

### Aug 1, 1975 — Helsinki Accords
- **Summary:** 35 nations recognize post-WWII borders — a Soviet gain. But human rights clauses (Basket III) become a lever for dissidents. Double-edged for the USSR.
- **Tension:** 4.5/10 (Moderate)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Nov 11, 1975 — Angola Proxy War
- **Summary:** USSR and Cuba back MPLA; US backs UNITA. 36,000 Cuban troops secure the Soviet-aligned government. Africa becomes a Cold War battleground.
- **Tension:** 5.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Oct 14, 1977 — SS-20 Missiles Deployed
- **Summary:** USSR deploys SS-20 intermediate-range missiles targeting Western Europe. European security balance shifts dramatically. NATO responds with Pershing deployments.
- **Tension:** 5.8/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Aug 12, 1978 — Sino-Japanese Treaty of Peace and Friendship
- **Summary:** Japan and China formalize a treaty that underlines China's outward balancing beyond the Soviet bloc. Triangular diplomacy further constrains Moscow.
- **Tension:** 5.1/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Sep 17, 1978 — Camp David Accords
- **Summary:** Egypt signs peace with Israel — pulling the Arab world's largest military out of Soviet orbit. A massive strategic loss for the USSR.
- **Tension:** 6.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Feb 1, 1979 — Iranian Revolution / Hostage Crisis
- **Summary:** Shah falls; Khomeini takes power. 52 US diplomats held hostage for 444 days. Radical Islamism threatens both superpowers.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Jun 18, 1979 — SALT II Signed but Shelved
- **Summary:** Carter and Brezhnev sign SALT II — then the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan kills ratification. Arms control framework collapses.
- **Tension:** 7.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Dec 12, 1979 — NATO Double-Track Decision
- **Summary:** NATO approves new missile deployments while offering arms-control negotiations, creating the framework for the Euromissile confrontation.
- **Tension:** 7.8/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Dec 25, 1979 — Soviet Invasion of Afghanistan
- **Summary:** Christmas 1979 — 100,000 Soviet troops invade. Brezhnev miscalculates badly. The Carter Doctrine declared. CIA arms the mujahideen. Détente is officially dead.
- **Tension:** 8.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Jan 4, 1980 — Carter Doctrine Announced
- **Summary:** The US declares Persian Gulf access a vital interest and signals military force if necessary. Soviet actions in Afghanistan trigger a wider strategic hardening.
- **Tension:** 7.6/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Aug 31, 1980 — Solidarity Agreements in Poland
- **Summary:** Workers secure legal recognition for Solidarity, creating an unprecedented independent labor movement inside the Eastern bloc.
- **Tension:** 7.0/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Sep 22, 1980 — Iran-Iraq War Begins
- **Summary:** Saddam invades Iran. Both superpowers maneuver. US eventually tilts toward Iraq. USSR sells weapons to both sides. The Persian Gulf is militarized.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Nov 4, 1980 — Reagan Elected — Second Cold War
- **Summary:** Reagan wins in a landslide. 'Evil Empire.' Defense spending doubles. B-1 bomber, MX missiles, 600-ship navy. Military Keynesianism reshapes the US economy.
- **Tension:** 8.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Dec 13, 1981 — Martial Law in Poland — Solidarity
- **Summary:** Jaruzelski declares martial law, crushing Solidarity under Soviet pressure. Reagan imposes economic sanctions. Pope John Paul II rallies Catholics worldwide.
- **Tension:** 7.8/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Nov 10, 1982 — Brezhnev Dies / Andropov Takes Power
- **Summary:** Brezhnev dies. Andropov (former KGB chief) succeeds him. Brief hopes for reform — but gravely ill from day one; dies 15 months later.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Mar 8, 1983 — Reagan's 'Evil Empire' Speech
- **Summary:** Reagan calls the USSR 'evil empire' and 'focus of evil in the modern world.' Soviets furious. Ideological war now alongside the geopolitical one.
- **Tension:** 8.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Mar 23, 1983 — SDI 'Star Wars' Announced
- **Summary:** Reagan proposes a space-based missile defense shield. If it works, it nullifies the Soviet deterrent. Moscow cannot match it economically. Deep alarm.
- **Tension:** 8.2/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** US

### Sep 1, 1983 — KAL 007 Shot Down — 269 Dead
- **Summary:** Soviet Su-15 shoots down Korean Air Lines 007 — 269 dead including a US congressman. Reagan: 'a crime against humanity.'
- **Tension:** 8.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Sep 26, 1983 — Soviet False Alarm Incident (Petrov)
- **Summary:** A Soviet early-warning system reports incoming US missiles; duty officer Stanislav Petrov judges it false, preventing dangerous escalation.
- **Tension:** 8.8/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Nov 2, 1983 — ABLE ARCHER 83 — Near Nuclear War
- **Summary:** NATO exercise so realistic Soviets believe an attack is imminent. Soviet nuclear forces go on full alert. British double-agent Gordievsky warns London. The most dangerous moment since 1962.
- **Tension:** 9.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Nov 20, 1983 — INF Euromissile Deployments Begin
- **Summary:** NATO starts deploying Pershing II and cruise missiles in Western Europe. Moscow suspends parts of arms-control dialogue and tensions spike.
- **Tension:** 8.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Feb 13, 1984 — Chernenko Takes Power
- **Summary:** Andropov dies. Chernenko — 72, emphysemic — takes power. The third Soviet leader in three years. Gerontocracy at its most dysfunctional.
- **Tension:** 8.0/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### May 8, 1984 — USSR Boycotts LA Olympics
- **Summary:** Soviet defector Gordievsky reveals the Kremlin genuinely feared a US first strike. RYAN operation monitors the US for attack signs around the clock.
- **Tension:** 8.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Mar 11, 1985 — Gorbachev Takes Power
- **Summary:** Mikhail Gorbachev, 54, becomes General Secretary. Youngest Soviet leader since Stalin. Unilateral nuclear test moratorium declared. Reagan cautiously intrigued.
- **Tension:** 7.5/10 (Critical)
- **Primary side:** USSR

### Nov 19, 1985 — Geneva Summit — Reagan & Gorbachev
- **Summary:** First Reagan-Gorbachev summit. No agreements reached but personal chemistry ignites. Famous fireside chat. Seeds of the end of the Cold War are planted.
- **Tension:** 6.5/10 (High)
- **Primary side:** Both

### Apr 26, 1986 — Chernobyl Disaster
- **Summary:** Reactor 4 explodes — worst nuclear accident in history. Gorbachev sees it as proof the Soviet system cannot be incrementally reformed. Glasnost dramatically accelerates.
- **Tension:** 6.0/10 (High)
